About

Neurotech@UPenn is a collection of computer scientists and neuroscience researchers inspired by the human brain and working to replicate its core principles on the path to AGI. Our focus is on developing brain‑inspired computational algorithms and architectures that translate insights from biological neural systems into scalable, general‑purpose AI.

We operate at the interface of brain circuits, neurotechnology, and modern AI. The group seeks to understand how real neural systems represent, learn, and compute, and to use these principles to inform attention mechanisms, reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF), and scalable architectures for sequence modeling, decision‑making, and control.

Mission

UPenn Prestige exists to make Penn a hub for brain-inspired computation — where ideas flow between systems neuroscience, neurotech labs, and cutting-edge AI research groups.

  • Build a rigorous, computation-first community around neural networks, brain-like systems, and systems neuroscience.
  • Design and analyze models that connect cortical circuits, attention, and RLHF with modern deep learning practice.
  • Create a bridge between neurotechnology platforms (BCIs, large-scale neural recordings) and CS/AI methods.
  • Support student-led research, reading groups, and collaborations that push towards scalable, interpretable, reliable AI systems.

Research Areas

The group is oriented around questions that naturally lie between computer science and neurotechnology: how neural circuits implement credit assignment, attention, memory, and control, and how these mechanisms can inform the next generation of neural network architectures and RLHF pipelines.

Brain-inspired deep learning Attention & transformers Reinforcement learning & RLHF Computational & systems neuroscience Neurotechnology & BCIs Alignment & safety primitives
  • Brain-inspired neural networks: Architectures that borrow from cortical circuits and recurrent dynamics, including attention-based and memory-augmented models.
  • Attention and representation: Comparing attention mechanisms in transformers with selective routing and population codes in real neural circuits.
  • Reinforcement learning & RLHF: Algorithms for credit assignment, preference modeling, and policy optimization informed by neural and behavioral data.
  • Neurotechnology as a testbed: Using BCIs and large-scale recordings as constraints and evaluation environments for models of perception, decision, and control.

Prestige Talks

Prestige Talks is a high-signal seminar and discussion series designed to be technically deep for CS/AI audiences while remaining grounded in real neural data and neurotech platforms.

  • Invited talks from researchers working on cortical circuit models, attention, and large-scale neural networks.
  • Sessions on RLHF, alignment, and how human feedback and brain signals can shape policy learning.
  • Whiteboard-heavy discussions and paper clubs bridging systems neuroscience, deep learning, and neuroengineering.
